Sustainable Development Goal 14

Life Below Water: Conserve and sustainably use the oceans, seas and marine resources for sustainable development.

Key Targets of SDG 14

The United Nations has outlined specific targets to guide global efforts towards achieving SDG 14. These targets address various aspects of marine conservation and sustainable use.

  • 14.1: By 2025, prevent and significantly reduce marine pollution of all kinds.
  • 14.2: By 2020, sustainably manage and protect marine and coastal ecosystems.
  • 14.3: Minimize and address the impacts of ocean acidification.
  • 14.4: By 2020, effectively regulate harvesting and end overfishing, IUU fishing, and destructive fishing practices.
  • 14.5: By 2020, conserve at least 10% of coastal and marine areas.
  • 14.6: By 2020, prohibit certain forms of fisheries subsidies which contribute to overcapacity and overfishing.
  • 14.7: By 2030, increase the economic benefits to SIDS and LDCs from the sustainable use of marine resources.
  • 14.A: Increase scientific knowledge, develop research capacity and transfer marine technology.
  • 14.B: Provide access for small-scale artisanal fishers to marine resources and markets.
  • 14.C: Enhance the conservation and sustainable use of oceans and their resources by implementing international law as reflected in UNCLOS.

Major Threats to Marine Ecosystems

Our oceans are facing unprecedented threats from human activities and climate change, endangering marine biodiversity and the critical services oceans provide.

Pollution

Plastic debris, chemical contaminants, oil spills, and nutrient runoff from agriculture and wastewater.

Overfishing

Depletion of fish stocks beyond sustainable levels, including illegal, unreported, and unregulated (IUU) fishing.

Climate Change

Ocean warming, acidification due to increased CO2 absorption, and sea-level rise impacting coastal habitats.

Habitat Destruction

Degradation of coastal ecosystems like mangroves and coral reefs due to development, dredging, and destructive fishing.

Pathways to Healthy Oceans

Addressing the challenges requires a multi-faceted approach involving policy changes, technological innovation, community engagement, and individual actions.

  • Establishing and effectively managing Marine Protected Areas (MPAs).
  • Transitioning to sustainable fishing practices and aquaculture.
  • Reducing plastic waste and improving waste management systems.
  • Investing in research and development of ocean-friendly technologies.
  • Implementing policies to reduce greenhouse gas emissions and combat climate change.
  • Promoting ocean literacy and public awareness.
